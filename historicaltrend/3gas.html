Gas Energy Trend

The graph highlights gas energy consumption per capita (measured in kWh) from 1990 to 2023. The United States leads in per capita gas usage, showing a consistent upward trend in recent decades, reflecting its substantial reliance on natural gas as a primary energy source. Countries like Russia and Saudi Arabia also maintain high levels of gas consumption, with some fluctuations over the years.

Emerging economies such as China exhibit a steady rise in gas consumption per capita, albeit at much lower levels compared to developed nations. This trend highlights the growing importance of gas as a transitional energy source in the shift toward lower-carbon energy systems. European nations, including the Netherlands and the United Kingdom, show moderate and relatively stable gas usage, reflecting their diversified energy portfolios.

These trends underscore the need for further investments in cleaner gas technologies and infrastructure while accelerating transitions to renewable energy sources. Balancing gas usage with carbon reduction targets will be a critical challenge for energy-intensive economies in the coming decades.
